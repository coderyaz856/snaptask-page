TickTick Alternative for Android: Free, Lightweight, No Account

May 3, 2026 · Quordesk Labs · 4 min read
Quick Answer

What is the best TickTick alternative for Android?

SnapTask is the lightest free option. It works fully offline, needs no account, and adds tasks from your Android notification bar in 3 taps. Free APK on Gumroad and APKPure. Under 4 MB, no ads, no premium upsell.

Why people search for a TickTick alternative

TickTick is a strong product. The reasons people end up looking for an alternative are usually not about quality but about fit:

  • Account is required before adding the first task
  • Premium subscription is needed for calendar view, advanced reminders, and filters
  • The app is heavier than the average task list needs
  • Tasks live on TickTick servers

For users who only need short, fast task capture, TickTick is more than they need.

What does a "TickTick alternative" actually need to do?

The honest list:

  1. Open in less than 1 second
  2. Let you write the task without thinking about which list, project, label, or priority
  3. Remind you at the right time
  4. Get out of the way

Other free TickTick alternatives

Microsoft To Do

Free, clean UI, but requires a Microsoft account and tends to feel slow for quick capture. Best if you already live in the Microsoft ecosystem.

Google Tasks

Minimal and free, but tightly tied to a Google account and Gmail. Adding a task still requires opening the app. No notification bar input.

Todoist (free)

Polished, well-designed, but the free tier is limited and reminders are paid. If you do not need a full project system, Todoist is overbuilt for your use case.

Markor or Orgzly

Both are open-source, fully offline, no account. Great for power users who want plain-text or org-mode workflows. Neither is built for quick capture from the notification bar.

How fast is task entry in SnapTask?

The full flow:

  1. Pull down your notification shade
  2. Tap the SnapTask input field
  3. Type the task — for example: Email landlord tomorrow
  4. Tap Send

That is it. About 3 seconds. The same speed as replying to a WhatsApp message from the notification bar.

Frequently asked questions

What is a good free TickTick alternative for Android?

SnapTask is the lightest free option, with no account, offline storage, and notification bar input. Microsoft To Do and Google Tasks are other free options if you accept a Microsoft or Google account.

Is TickTick free or paid?

TickTick has a free tier and a Premium subscription at around $35 per year. Calendar view, custom filters, and advanced reminders are gated behind Premium.

Why look for a TickTick alternative?

Common reasons: account required, premium gating, heavy app, and the realization that you only need quick task capture, not a full project management system.

Is SnapTask offline?

Yes. SnapTask stores all tasks locally with no cloud sync and no account.

Does SnapTask have a calendar view?

Not yet. SnapTask is intentionally minimal: a list of tasks with reminders. A calendar view is on the roadmap but not in the current version.
